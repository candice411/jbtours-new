NAMIBIAN TRAIN TOURS

We’ve just returned from Namibia (July 2011) after visiting some of our service providers in the neighbouring country and we would like to confirm that after the good rains in Namibia during the past rain season the ‘veld’ is lush and the game is in good condition. Namibia is this year in its splendid best! The ‘more than normal raining’ season has changed dry river beds into free flowing rivers. This year is THE year to visit Namibia.

Tour No. 1 (14th to 24th Aug 2011) is already fully booked, whilst Tour No 2. (26th Aug to 4th Sept 2011) still has space available More info – even some En Suite compartments are still available. See our Special offer on Tour No. 2 below.

We’ll be hosting again two Namibian train tours during 2011, taking place in August andSeptember of this year, departing from Jo’burg. Both tours are 10 days long and visit popular tourist destinations like: Fish River Canyon, Windhoek, Etosha, Swakopmund, Spitzkoppe, Giant’s Playground, Quiver Tree Forest, a wine estate(!) and the Namib Desert. One sleeps every night on the heritage train and two meals per day are included in the tour package, whilst daily excursions are undertaken by buses. Experienced tour guides travel along.

Last minute special offer: 50% discount on partner’s tour price. I.e.: First person pay in full and partner pays only 50% of the tour price.

Table Mountain Aerial Cableway announces new rates

The Table Mountain Aerial Cableway has announced its new rates, valid from 1 October 1 to September, 2012. Online tickets will cost R85 for children return and R43 one-way. A return pass for adults is R175 and R92 one-way. Ticket office rates for children are R95 return and R50 one-way. Adult rates are R195 return and R100 one-way. Concession rates are valid only on Fridays, where South African senior citizens pay R95 for a return pass and R50 one-way. Student rates are R125 return and R65 one-way. The cableway has also announced an update of its operating times, which are available on the Table Mountain Aerial Cableway website.http://www.tablemountain.net

DAY TRAIN TRIPS

There are only two companies in Gauteng & one in KZN hosting day trips by train. This is ideal for families, companies, churches, clubs, etc. and especially for children who have never been on a train trip. Both these companies haul the train with a steam locomotive engine!

Pretoria area: Friends of the Rail Contact Mr. Arno Victor at Tel: 082 293 4616 or sales@friendsoftherail.com or visit their website at http://www.friendsoftherail.com They travel mostly from Pretoria to Cullinan & Bela Bela..

Johannesburg area: Reef Steamers Contact Mr. Theunis van Wyk at Tel: 011 025 4363 or 072 440-5293 or bookings@reefsteamers.com or visit their website at http://www.reefsteamers.com They travel mostly from Maraisburg to Magaliesburg.

KZN (Durban & PMB) area: Umgeni Steam Rail Contact Mr. Rob McGregor at Tel: 031-303 3003; 082-353 6003 or 087-808 7715 or robbie@umgenisteamrailway.co.za or visit their website at http://www.umgenisteamrailway.co.za/. They travel mostly from Inchanga, Kloof & PMB.

Cape Town area: Atlantic Rail Contact them at Tel: (021) 556-1012 or info@atlanticrail.co.za or visit their website at http://www.atlanticrail.co.za They travel mostly from Cape Town station to close by destinations in the Peninsula and Boland.

P.M.E.S. The Pietermaritzburg Model Engineering Society operates miniature live steam trains for the public on the first Sunday of each month at their site in Bisley, PIETERMARITZBURG. Contact Andries Keyser at 082 557 2119
