QUA VADIS LONG DISTANCE TRAINS in SOUTH AFRICA? Follow up

The articles in the October & November 2010 Newsletters, about the suspension of long distance passenger trains in South Africa for more than 3 months (12 Aug to end of Nov 2010) caused an interesting outcry from many readers. Some blamed JB Train Tours for it whilst others blamed the government or Transnet (and even Spoornet and Metrorail!) or SA Tourism, etc. Fact is, whoever is to be blamed caused a lot of injury to many role players in the industry. However, the biggest "injury" was caused to passengers who wanted to travel by rail. Whether by going home, to work or just on holiday.

On Wednesday 17th November 2010, JB Train Tours attended a workshop hosted by Shosholoza Meyl to be informed about the current train scenario, the future of long distance trains in South Africa and Rail Tourism per se. According to acting CEO of Shosholoza Meyl, Mr Paul M. Zikhali, ALL servives will be 'back to normal' as from 1st December 2010. Though, certain routes will be served by combined trains. I.e.: Tourist Class and Economy Class will share the same train.

This announcement will have no immediate affect on our December 2010 tours from Jo'burg to Cape Town, because all the originally scheduled train tours have been changed to luxury bus tours and accommodation & services en route have been booked and paid.

We (JB Train Tours) will update our 2011 Year Programme accordingly - to be available soon.

Last minute note: The trains did NOT start running on the 1st December 2010!
 
KURT DARREN se 2011 inniBosveld FEES - Naboomspruit

Die eerste inniBosveld Fees was in Februarie 2010 op Kurt Darren (bekende en gewilde sanger) se wildsplaas buite Naboomspruit aangebied. Die organiseerders het gehoop & vertrou dat daar 5000 mense sou opdaag. Daar daag toe wel bykans 18000 op! Dit het talle nuwe eise aan die organiseerders gestel en 2011 se fees wat oor 2 dae (18 & 19 Februarie) aangebied word, sal ietwat anders daar uitsien, PLUS al die tekortkominge van die 2010 Fees sal aangespreek word.

Ons maatskappy is weer versoek om die bustoere soontoe te reël en het onlangs weer op Kurt se plaas gaan kuier & vergader om ons van die nuutste logistieke reëlings te vergewis. Ons het een bus vir 2010 beplan & begroot en daar het toe ses (6) busse gery! Vir baie mense was dit die maklikste, veiligste, gerieflikste en lekkerste wyse om die Fees by te woon. Ons reël vir 2011 weer die bustoere soontoe.

JB Train Tours bied in 2011 ook drie verskillende naweek-buspakkette na die Fees toe aan. Die busse vertrek op Vrydagoggend 18 Feb 2011 vanaf Jo'burg lughawe (ORTIA) en reis oor Centurion en Pretoria na die onderskeie bestemmings in en buite Naboomspruit. Passasiers kan langs die roete (Centurion, Menlopark en Montana in Pretoria) ook opklim.

Die naweek-bustoerpakkette sluit retoer-busvervoer, 2 nagte akkommodasie, 2 etes per dag, toegang tot beide die Feesdae en 'n toergids in. Met die een toerpakket word in die skoolkoshuis op Naboomspruit oornag. Die tweede toerpakket maak voorsiening vir verblyf in luukse tente by Die Oog minerale bron, terwyl die derde pakket verblyf in chalets op 'n wildsplaas verseker. Al drie hierdie toer-opsies gaan heerlike kuiergeleenthede en waarde vir geld bied. Akkommodasie in die omgewing is MIN oor hierdie naweek. Meer inligting oor die 3 naweekpakkette.

Die dagtoer-buspakkette sal ook weer aangebied word. Daar word op Vrydag- en Saterdagoggende vanuit JHB & PTA vertrek.  Kostes beloop R450 pp (Vrydag) en R500 pp (Saterdag). Meer inligting oor die dagpakkette.

'n Verdere nuwigheid is dat daar in 2011 op beide Feesdae 'n middag-voorprogram aangebied word waartydens minder-bekende kunstenaars sal optree. Diegene wat vroeg arriveer sal derhalwe ook ekstra kyk- en luistergenot ervaar.

Die kunstenaars wat gaan optree lyk soos die 'Who's who' van Afrikaanse musiek in SA. Besoek gerus ook die Fees webwerf by www.innibosveld.co.za

SMILE A MOMENT

Notices seen:

  1.  In a laundromat: Automatic washing machines. Please remove all your clothes when the light goes out.
  2. In an office: Would the person who took the step ladder yesterday kindly bring it back or further steps will be taken.
  3. In another office: After the tea break, staff should empty the teapot and stand upside down on the draining board.
  4. On a church door: This is the gate of Heaven. Enter ye all by this door. (This door is kept locked because of the draft. Please use side entrance.)
  5. Outside a second-hand shop: We exchange anything - bicycles, washing machines etc. Why not bring your wife along and get a wonderful bargain?
  6. Quicksand warning: Quicksand. Any person passing this point will be drowned. By order of the District Council.
  7. Notice in a dry cleaner's window: Anyone leaving their garments here for more than 30 days will be disposed of.
  8. In a health food shop window: Closed due to illness.
  9. Spotted in a safari park: Elephants Please Stay In Your Car.
  10. Seen during a conference: For anyone who has children and doesn't know it, there is a day care on the first floor.
  11. Notice in a field: The farmer allows walkers to cross the field for free, but the bull charges.
  12. Message on a leaflet: If you cannot read, this leaflet will tell you how to get lessons.
  13. On a repair shop door: We can repair anything (Please knock hard on the door - the bell doesn't work.)
  14. Spotted in a toilet in an office block: Toilet out of order. Please use floor below.
