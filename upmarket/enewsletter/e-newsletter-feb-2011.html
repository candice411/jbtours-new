NEW TRAIN ROSTURES

The suspension of all long distance passenger trains in South Africa in August 2010 has lead to a number of consequences for passengers and Rail Tourism operators alike.

On the negative side it meant that passengers had no train services for many months and were negatively affected by this action. Now lesser Tourist Class beds per train would be available as well.

On the positive side are the following:

1.         The frequency of the trains running has increased: As from January 2011.
The Trans Karoo train between Jo’burg and Cape Town will now run 7 days per week compared to only 3 times per week last year
The Trans Natal train between Jo’burg and Durban will now run 6 days per week compared to only once per week last year
The Algoa train between Jo’burg and Port Elizabeth will now run 6 days per week compared to only 2 times per week last year
The Trans Orange train between Cape Town and Durban (via Kimberley & Bloemfontein) will still run only once per week but departing Cape Town on a Monday and departing Durban on a Wednesday, compared to Wednesday and Friday in 2010.

2.         The number of beds available per train has dropped:
All these trains mentioned above will only have three (3) Tourist Class carriages per train, resulting in only 84 beds per train. It means only 6 x 2-bed coupe’s per train.

3.         Lesser people using the dining car:
The number of people using the dining car during peak hours should be substantially less.

4.         The sleeper carriages are ‘re-done’:
In practice it means that currently ALL sleeper carriages are inspected, checked, repaired and re-authorised as being safe and ready-to-go before using again on any route.

For JB Train Tours it means more opportunities available to host tours on other departure days BUT much more careful planning when multiple trains-per-tour are used.

The above arrangements would remain as is until further notice.
