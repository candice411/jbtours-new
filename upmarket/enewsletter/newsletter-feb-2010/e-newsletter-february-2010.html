KURT DARREN se inniBOSVELD MUSIEKFEES

Op Saterdag 20 Februarie 2010, een dag na Kurt Darren (bekende en baie gewilde Afrikaanse sanger) se verjaarsdag, bied hy ‘n Afrikaanse musiekfees op sy wildsplaas buite Naboomspruit in Limpopo aan. Die organiseerders van die Fees het JB Train Tours versoek om bustoere te reël om mense vanaf Gauteng vir die dag soontoe te neem.

Die bustoere werk op dieselfde basis as die Huisgenoot Skouspel se bustoere wat elke jaar in Oktober na die Super Bowl by Sun City gaan: Die toerpakket sluit die retoer busrit per luukse bus, toegangskaartjie, kampstoeltjie en toergids in en die busse vertrek vanaf die Oos- & Wes-Rand, asook vanaf Centurion en Pretoria. Die toerpakket kos R400 pp en laerskoolkinders betaal R330. Voorskoolse kinders, wat op hul ouers se skote sit, reis gratis terwyl iemand wat op die 19de of 20ste Februarie verjaar, R100 afslag kry.

15 Afrikaanse musieksterre tree saam met Kurt op wanneer die 4-uur lange opvoering in ‘n buitelug-teater aangebied word. Kontak ons NOU om plek te bespreek. Meer inligting
 
CAPE TOWN JAZZ FESTIVAL

The 11th annual Cape Town Jazz Festival will be hosted again over the coming Easter weekend in Cape Town. More than 40 local and international artists will be performing in the Cape Town International Conventions Centre. In 2009 more than 32000 people attended.

JB Train Tours have organised a train tour to this event on short notice with (unfortunately) limited space available. The tour departs on Easter Friday, 2nd April 2010 from Jo’burg and will arrive back on Tuesday, 6th April 2010. Accommodation is in Cape Town in a top hotel across the street from the Jazz Festival, meals, as well as the Entry Passto the two-day Jazz festival are included in the tour package. Tour price is R3600 pp sharing. More info
 
DID YOU KNOW?
  • That in May 2010 it would be 100 years since the South African Railways has been formed
  • That in June 2010 it would be 150 years of public railways in South Africa
  • That the Bluff Railway (in Durban) of 1.5 km was operational a year before the Durban to Point railway was completed
  • That we’ve had our 500000th visitor to the JB Train Tours’ website in the beginning of January 2010. This is since Oct 2004. It means it is close to 8500 visits per month on average
  • That we have another biker riding his 3rd Western Cape Motorbike-on-Train tour. Obviously he can’t get enough of a good thing (and a beautiful area) in our country
  • That some passengers who went on the train tour with us to Cape Town in December 2009, have already booked again for December 2010! It looks like the people from Limpopo DO like the Cape.
  • That the Durban International Airport will be officially and permanently being closed on Friday 30th April 2010. As from 1st May 2010 all flights will land and depart from the new King Shaka International airport, 52 km north of Durban, close to La Lucia.

NAMIBIA in May 2010

We’ll be hosting only two train tours from Jo’burg to Namibia during 2010, compared to the 5 tours we did in 2009. This is mainly due to the unavailability of the train set due to the Soccer World Cup to be hosted in South Africa this year.

Namibia had exceptionally good rain during the summer and the veld is lush and green. Even the dessert looks different compared to other seasons.

Both tours are again 10 days long and are scheduled for 19 – 28 May (4 spaces left in Emerald Class) & 31 May to 9 June 2010.
 
BUFFALO RALLY TRAIN TOUR

The first Buffalo Rally for bikers (visit www.nomads.org.za) was held in 1969 at Bathurst in the Eastern Cape. +-250 Bikers attended. In 2010 the ‘Buff’ will be hosted again at Santos/De Bakke municipal resorts in Mossel Bay – this time many 1000’s of bikers will attend.

To make life easier for bikers from ‘up country’, JB Train Tours has organised a train trip for bikers (with their bikes and pillions with them on the train) from Jo’burg to Beaufort West. After off loading they’ll ride the 253 km to Mossel Bay via spectacular Meiringspoort & over the Robinson Pass.

The tour is from Wednesday 17th March to Monday 22nd March 2010 (Monday is a Public Holiday) and the tour price is R3100 pp. The pillion pays less. More info
