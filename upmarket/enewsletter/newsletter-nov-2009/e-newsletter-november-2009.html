TRAIN DELAYS - ALL TRAINS
It DOES happen, unfortunately, that trains are delayed en route. (Just like aeroplanes, buses, cars, taxi’s, etc.) This result in passengers arriving late at their destinations; passengers getting irritated and more time is spent on the train than planned. However, when a train (or trains) is delayed, Shosholoza Meyl and all their partners and service providers do their utmost to get everything back to normal as quickly as possible. No person ON the train can make the train go faster if there are technical problems. NOT the Train Manager, NOT the waiter in the dining car, NOT the tour operator and NOT the train driver. One just has to wait until everything has been addressed and solved by the off-board technical staff coming from afar.

Cable theft next to railway lines is probably the biggest reason for trains running late. If cables are stolen it means that the signal system doesn’t work, resulting in ‘a red robot situation’ for the train. I.e.: The train has to stop at every signal post and get permission telephonically to proceed.

Note: During the launch of Transport Month (October) it was announced that 190 diesel locomotives would be on standby during the 2010 Soccer World Cup Tournament in South Africa for in case electrical or mechanical failures do occur. (Why only then? – Ed.)
 
TOY RUN in NOVEMBER – COUNTRY WIDE
The annual ‘Toy Run’ will be hosted again on Sunday 29th November 2009 in several places in different regions in South Africa. The main purpose is for bikers to get together, ride together and to donate a toy to less preveledged children.

In 2008 some 55000 bikers departed from different venues and drove to a gathering place in their area, dropping the toy at a collection point. Afterwards fun and music was the order of the day.

The 2009 Gauteng Toy Run will depart from many different venues (I.e.: Pretoria, East Rand, West Rand, Centurion, etc.) in the morning at about 09:00 and all bikes will proceed in convoy to the MTN Expo Centre at Nasrec. Hence, if you are on the road that morning somewhere in Gauteng, expect to see 1000’s of bikes roaring past.

JB Train Tours will be hosting a stall at the Expo, promoting their ‘Motorbike-on-Train’ tours BUT making a contribution to welfare as well. A competition will be run where any person can win a trip on one of the ‘Motorbike-on-Train’ tours to be hosted in 2010. If you’re reading this article and you are going to the Gauteng Toy Run, please pop in at our stall and say ‘hello’!
