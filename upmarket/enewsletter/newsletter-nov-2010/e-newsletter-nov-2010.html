QUA VADIS LONG DISTANCE TRAINS in SOUTH AFRICA? Follow up

The article in the October 2010 Newsletter lead to several enquiries from concerned travellers about the future of train tours in South Africa. We at JB Train Tours are still very enthusiastic about Rail Tourism, but whether Shosholoza Meyl shares this view is doubtful. Not only Rail Tourism, but the running of long distance trains, is in jeopardy.

The contradictive statements made and the lack of information supplied by Shosholoza Meyl, make it virtually impossible to plan for the future. A recent press release stated that "trains are running normal again', but if one goes to any station there is no Tourist Class train ticket to be bought nor any train! Recently a senior manager at Shosholoza Meyl informed us that NO Tourist Class train will run for the rest of 2010 - including during the peak period. After we've changed all eleven of our December 2010 train tours from Jo'burg to Cape Town to luxury coach tours, affecting about 1300 people, the same manager denied that he ever said it!! (No, they did not put anything in writing).

Currently our company is playing a 'wait and see' game. JB Train Tours is Shosholoza Meyl's biggest client, booking annually the most number of passengers on their trains, but we are not treated accordingly. All this is affecting YOU, the client, because we can't communicate correct up-to-date information to you.

However, we are STILL committed to Rail Tourism and we trust that the authorities will get their act together and serve the country as a whole - very soon. The good news is that the Premier Classe train between Jo'burg and Cape Town is still running regularly.

TWO OCEANS MARATHON 2011 - ENTRIES OPEN 1 NOVEMBER

We open entries on Monday 1 November and are extremely excited about all on offer for the 2011 event. This year (2010) over 27 000 participants took part in the various distances including the fun runs, making it the biggest running event in Africa. Our flagship race, the ultra marathon, attracted 9 000 competitors, while the half marathon saw 11 700 runners take part – a limited field in the interests of runner safety as per advice from the City's Disaster Management team, but it still remains the biggest half marathon in Africa. Entries had to close off four weeks prior to the scheduled closing date, which meant that we turned away about 3 000 disappointed runners. Remember to enter early, particularly for the half marathon, to avoid missing out.  http://www.twooceansmarathon.org.za See our Ad on their website

As for the past 10 years, the annual Two Oceans train tour will be running over the Easter Weekend from Jo'burg to Cape Town for runners and supporters to be able to attend this momentous occasion on the running calendar. More info See our Ad on their website as well as on the Entry Form.
 
DIESELFDE LEMOEN - SOET of SUUR?

Baie van ons lesers het altemit al die paradoks gehoor van 'n lemoen wat in twee helftes gesny word. Dit word dan vir twee verskillende mense gegee. Die een persoon sê dat dit 'n heerlike sappige en soet lemoen is, terwyl die ander een sê dit is suur en droërig!

So gaan dit ook met ons toere. Sommige mense beskryf 'n toer as' fantasties, lekker, goed georganiseerd, uitstekende gids, smaaklike kos, ens.' terwyl 'n ander persoon op dieselfde toer weer net die teenoorgestelde sal sê. Hieronder is twee voorbeelde van e-posse wat ons ontvang het van twee toergangers wat op dieselfde bus na vanjaar se Huisgenoot Skouspel by Sun City gereis het.

Die ou vraag is weer: "Hoe kan ons elke persoon al die tyd tevrede stel?!" Feit is, ons maatskappy doen ons bes om die meerderheid mense se reisbehoeftes die meerderheid van die tyd te help bevredig.
