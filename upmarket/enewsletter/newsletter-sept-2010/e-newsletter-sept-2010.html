TEMPORARY SUSPENSION of LONG DISTANCE TRAINS in SOUTH AFRICA

It is common knowledge that all longs distance trains in South Africa were suspended by Shosholoza Meyl on Wednesday the 11th August 2010 – without any pre-warning – for 2 weeks. Shosholoza Meyl forms part of ‘Prasa’ (Passenger Rail Agency of South Africa) which is a parastatal of the Government. The suspension derived from a dispute between Prasa and Transnet about the maintenance of locomotives (hauling the trains).

The unfortunate result was that only a limited number of workable/operational locomotives were available, leading to trains being cancelled on short notice, leading to paid-up PRASA passengers being stranded at stations. In some cases alternative bus transport was made available to passengers. All these cancellations affected JB Train Tours’ tours during August 2010 negatively. At least 13 of our tours had to be re-scheduled, re-organised and re-vamped – all on VERY short notice. Only one tour could not be re-organised (The rugby train tour from JHB to CPT). The time limited and unavailability of resources prevented us from doing so.

Obviously the re-arranged tours did not suit everyone’s individual needs, but we have had two choices about these tours:Firstly: We could have cancelled the tours (the easy way out), informed the passengers accordingly, wait for all the complaints, moans and groans, respond to it and do all the refunds OR Secondly: We could try to salvage all the tours to the best of our ability with the resources available & service providers willing to assist. We have decided on the latter. AND WE ARE VERY PROUD ON WHAT WE’VE ACHIEVED.

It took lots of extra work (including after hours), lateral thinking, fruitful negotiations with service providers (airlines, bus companies, hotels, etc.) to make it happen. One should keep in mind that re-organising 45 passengers per tour to change travel arrangements form a train to a flight or bus does not happen quickly. The JB Train Tours team worked non-stop to make this happen.

Our appreciation and thanks to everyone who has sent us e-mails & faxes, as well as telephone calls, with kind words of appreciation for making the tours continue. At this very moment (end of August 2010) trains are NOT back to normal, except for the Premier Classe on certain routes, despite press releases by Shosholoza Meyl to that extend as well as articles in newspapers.

JB Train Tours are still very committed to Rail Tourism in South Africa and will continue to be involved and make it happen as long as it is practical possible.
