JB Train Tours has gone live in more ways than one!
 

JB Train Tours have been organising tour packages since 1975. We have been hosting train tours for the budget market for many years to various destinations in Southern Africa, but has identified the need to host shorter train tours for corporate companies. Our target market has always been for the normal guy on the street who can afford a well deserved holiday once a year but it has come to our attention that the need for upmarket tours is huge and that there is a generation of people who would like to experience a train trip in luxury.

We are very excited about the launch of our upmarket train tour packages, the launch of our website, the launch of our e-newsletter and the opportunity to be of service to more South Africans.
 
 
You will notice that our upmarket packages are very affordable and competitive which means this is definitely an option you want to consider for your next strategic session, ‘bos-beraad’, Executive meeting, Incentive trip or even your year end function in 2009!

We are looking forward to meet you ON the train....on your way to Durban or Cape Town.

Launch of JB Train Tours Upmarket Train Tour Packages – 14 November 2008
 

JB Train Tours (Since 1975) has launched its Upmarket Train Tour Packages on the luxurious Premier Classe train on Friday 14th November 2008 at the Johannesburg Park railway station in front of a well represented audience from the media, corporate representatives, event management companies, travel agencies and dignitories. The MC of the day, Mr. Kobus Barnard, welcomed the delegates and dignitaries.

Mr. Pumi Vani, Manager: Facilities and Security at Shosholoza Meyl, delivered the Word of Introduction, whilst Mr. Matti Geldenhuys, action CEO of Shosholoza Meyl, spoke about the future of the Premier Classe trains in South Africa.

Mr. Johan Badenhorst, CP in P at JB Train Tours, gave a brief presentation about the Upmarket Train Tour Packages offered by the company.

Afterwards, everyone present had the opportunity to walk through the Premier Classe train, parked at the station, to view the dining cars and kitchen, lounge cars with bar facilities and sleeper carriages with 2-bed compartments and 1-bed coupes.

Mr. Badenhorst has expressed his opinion that these upmarket train tour packages will address the needs of many event management companies and corporates alike, which are often ‘looking for something different to do’.

2009 plans – do you want to spoil your clients with a luxury train experience?

 

The world wide recession has hit South Africa as well. Many companies are cutting on their entertainment, training, events and marketing costs, BUT still have to do it to maintain market share and progress.

We suggest that if you would like to cut costs and still want to maintain the above mentioned activities to seriously consider travelling by luxury train.

The Premier Classe train compares very favourably with other more luxurious trains in South Africa, but at a fraction of the price PLUS we’ll assist with all the other arrangements. I.e.: Conference, transport off the train and excursions to interesting places.

The feedback we’ve received from our customers after the first number of tours on the Premier Classe, were overwhelming positive. From “Our expectations were exceeded” to a straight forward “This is the way to experience something different, whilst still very stylish and affordable”.

Our company has planned various ‘purpose orientated’ weekend tours from Johannesburg to Durban – ideally suited for smaller groups. I.e.: Entertainment for a small group of guests. Amongst other, it will include trips to the annual ‘July’ horse race, Duzi Canoe marathon, Comrades marathon, Motorbike Expo, Tourism Indaba and many more.

One of these train tours will bring new life into your annual events calendar
 

We can also book your transport-only trips

 

We have received a number of queries from people who only want to book a one-way or return train trip for himself or his family. We can do the bookings on your behalf for 6 or more passengers – at NO extra costs to you or your company.

Currently, the Premier Classe runs twice a week between Jo’burg and Cape Town, as well as between Jo’burg and Durban, and soon once a week between Cape Town and Port Elizabeth.

The ticket prices vary according to the time of the year. I.e.: Peak season or low season.
