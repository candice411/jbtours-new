THE GOOD OLD DAYS.... RELIVE IT

Good day xxx

When we look back with nostalgia on our youth, dreaming of what we may one day aspire to become, many a memory could be of becoming a train driver. The excitement of starting up that locomotive, guiding a long train towards whatever distance and adventure lay ahead has been a source of fascination and intrigue for budding train enthusiasts ...but so few have ever realised this dream.

However, the fascination of travelling by train is still imminent with many people – dreaming about traversing the Karoo in the late afternoon with the sun setting in the west and the small villages passing by whilst the train is speeding ahead.

It is fortunately still possible to live the latter dream. You will be spoilt for choice when you place your booking with us on the luxurious Premier Classe train on a packaged tour or a trip-only booking between major centres across Southern Africa.
 

Many corporate companies and individuals alike are making more and more use of this means of travel – just to be different and to enjoy!

We look forward to hosting you on board our Premier Classe train tours in the near future.

INTERESTING INFORMATION ABOUT A SPECIFIC TRAIN ROUTE
Johannesburg to Cape Town
Cape Town
 
Kimberley
 

One of our most popular (and oldest) routes on the Premier Classe train, is the route between Johannesburg (Jo’burg or Jozi) and Cape Town (the Mother City). This distance of + 1 500km (1 519 km to be exact) between the two cities is covered in 25 hours & 16 minutes.

The train only departs from Jo’burg (with no option for departure from PTA as many people still think) and runs via Potchefstroom & Klerksdorp (North West province), then via Bloemhof, Christiana and then finally onto Kimberly and De Aar which are both situated in the Northern Cape Province. The train enters the Western Cape Province, just after passing through Three Sisters en route to Beaufort West, Leeu Gamka, Laingsburg, Touwsriver, Worcester, Wellington and eventually into Cape Town.

One of the benefits and features of travelling by train on this route is that you’ll have time available to sit back and relax and to observe the smaller towns and surrounding areas passing by. A long and tiresome journey by car leaves little to notice and admire, take in the glorious picturesque frames of captured beauty of our country from the comfort of a coach window.

NAMIBIA
Lots of places to visit and lots of photos to take

“Have you ever been to Namibia” we often ask the 40+ of age gentlemen when addressing a gathering. “Yes”, they’ll respond, “when I was in the army on the border!”

Namibia is a beautiful country, full of contrast, interesting people, good beef and spectacular sunsets. Their food is divine and when they want to eat vegetables, they eat chicken (the saying goes!)

The 10-day train tour hosted by JB Train Tours is always very popular. In 2009 three tours will be hosted – one of which is fully booked. The two tours in April still have space available.

Some of the interesting places visited, include the only commercial wine cellar in Namibia (yes, wine cellar), located in Omaruru. A visit to the Kristall Galerei in Swakopmund is ‘obligatory’, whilst the beach ‘braai’ on an island outside Walvis Bay harbour is something not to be missed. A marquee with champagne and oysters served makes for a truly unforgettable event.

Obviously many of the well known and popular tourist destinations are visited, like the Fish River Canyon, Etosha Pans, Henties Bay and Spitzkoppe...but like we’ve said before, we only want to mention a few.

Many corporate companies have booked places on these tours as incentives for their staff (instead of sending them overseas) or service awards of achievement This tour is also very popular with photographers as they take the most interesting and category winning photos in Namibia. Have a look at the great photos below that was taken by our photographer, Lenie Badenhorst.

Fact is, travelling by train tour to Namibia is currently the most affordable way of getting there and touring the country. Do it!

MOTORBIKE-ON-TRAIN TOUR
26 Mountain passes, + 1 100 km...
 

Bikers have an unwavering following when it comes to their bikes.  They command at least a little space in a garage (yes please, not outside!) and occasionally pushed out for gleaming wipe down before a nice long breakfast run... in summer that is!  It’s time to dust off your beauty and bring her on board!

Many bikers, up-country (I.e.: Gauteng and surroundings) have done all the local roads and routes. They’ve been to Hartbeespoort Dam, Cullinan, Brits, Magaliesburg and even further a field to the Lowveld (or Eastern Transvaal as called by the bikers).

JB Train Tours initiated a 7-day Motorbike-on-Train tour in 2007 from Gauteng to the Western Cape & Boland on the Trans Karoo Tourist train. To build on the success of these tours (5 hosted per annum) a Motorbike-on-Train tour on the Premier Classe train has been organised as well.

The routes covered in the Cape are different to the ones being covered whilst travelling on the Tourist train, but all the beautiful passes and scenic routes (all on tar) are still enjoyed.

This tour is ideal for bikers that would like to travel in style. Give it a go!
