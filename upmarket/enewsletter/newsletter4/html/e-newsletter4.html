The first tour is departing on the 31st of March 2009 where after the second one will depart on the 12th of April and the third one on 15th of May 2009. 

Herewith an extract from Day 4 on the itinerary:

Eroded over millennia, the Fish River Canyon is the second largest natural gorge in the world. Set in a harsh, stony plain, dotted with drought resistant succulents such as the distinctive Quiver Tree or ‘Kokerboom’, the canyon is a spectacular natural phenomenon. It took hundreds of millions of years to evolve into its current shape.

Its full length is 160km, the width up to 27 km and the depth 550 m. After a quick stopover at the famous Canon Roadhouse, which is interestingly decorated with old car parts found in the region, we push further to Keetmanshoop for lunch. In the afternoon we visit Giant’s Playground, an impressive jumble of massive dolerite boulders of 160 – 180 million years old.

We end our afternoon with a visit to the Quiver Tree Forest, where around 300 specimens of this prehistoric tree, Aloe dichotoma, can be found.

 
They grow between 3 and 5 meters tall and their forked branches reach skywards, affording beautiful photo opportunities.
(Full day: distance traveled: ±300km)

INTERESTING INFORMATION ABOUT A SPECIFIC TRAIN ROUTE
Johannesburg to Durban
 

When traveling by car from Jo’burg to Durban it is very likely that you’ll travel on the N3 highway. Towns like Heidelberg, Warden, Harrismith, Mooi River and Pietermaritzburg would be seen from a distance and you’ll probably stop once or twice at a venue to fill up your car with fuel or visit a toilet.

Places like Vrede, Cornelia, Ladysmith, Estcourt and various other small towns won’t exist in your frame of reference when passing by. The trip will probably take you 6 to 8 hours – pending the traffic, fog on Van Reenens Pass, a lorry accident somewhere, striking toll gate operators, a speed fine or road maintenance.

When traveling by train it will take you 13 hours (overnight). You’ll sleep well, have two good meals en route, see various small towns en route, travel through 26 tunnels (the majority between Pietermaritzburg and Durban in the early morning of the trip), over long bridges, see valleys and waterfalls that you never knew exist and arrive fresh in the Holiday City.

Rental car facilities are available in Durban or when traveling in a group we’ll organise bus or coach transport on your behalf.

The Premier Classe train between JHB & DBN is ideally suited for companies that would like to have a one-day meeting, strategic session or conference in Durban during the week. (The train departs on a Tuesday evening at 18:20 from JHB and arrives back on Thursday morning at 06:30 in JHB.)

For those companies or individuals who’d like to travel over the weekend and spend one night in Durban, the train departs on a Friday evening at 18:20 from JHB and arrives back on Monday morning at 06:30 in JHB.
