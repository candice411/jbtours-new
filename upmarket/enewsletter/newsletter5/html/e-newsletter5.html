THE YOUNGER GENERATION HAS SOME CATCH UP TO DO

In our April 2009 e-zine Newsletter we’ve touched on the issue of travelling affordably in South Africa by train and we’ve published an article about companies selling our products (train tours) – especially to locals.

For many of the older generation (45+ years of age) travelling by train in their younger years was common practice. Either going to school, boarding school, varsity or on the December holidays at the sea. The younger generation (under 45) has not had the privilege of sleeping on an overnight train.
 

Our company’s budget and upmarket train tours give the opportunity to everyone to relive those nostalgic days – make use of it this year or start planning for next year.

We are looking forward in welcoming you on the platform and on the train.

Travel Greetings
Johan Badenhorst & the JB Train Tours team

JB TRAIN TOURS GOT OUR HANDS WET
Colgate Race water point sponsored by JB Train Tours
4 year old Zané working ‘’hard’’
 

The annual Colgate Run, hosted by Boksburg Athletic Club on Sunday, 26 April 2009 attracted more than 3 300 runners this year.

One of the exciting aspects to the runners is always the water points en route. Because JB Train Tours are well known in the running communities due to the annual Comrades & Two Oceans Train Tours hosted by us, we decided to sponsor one of the water points that served both the 15km & 32km routes.

A total of 77 cases of Coke, 14 000 water sachets and the JB Train Tours team of 22 people who included JB Train Tours Staff, family & friends (see the youngest member of 4 years old on the left), netball teams from Laerskool Baanbreker and Hoërskool Oosterlig respectively all contributed to a day of BIG fun.

 

    

The JB Train Tours water point has also won the best-water-point-prize this year (this is the second year in a row) due to the festive atmosphere that was created with music, boerewors rolls, t-shirts, a gazebo and great attitudes.
