THE RECESSION: HOW IS IT AFFECTING YOUR HOLIDAY PLANS?

The announcements during the past week that South Africa is now officially in a recession, has lead to a variety of shocks to the normal guy on the street. Some people have indicated that they ‘knew’ it a long time ago, whilst others still tried to ignore it.

Fact is, it is affecting South Africans across the board. The buying of luxury items and the spending habits on unnecessary goods has decreased substantially in the majority of house holds. The leisure industry in the country has been hit hard as well. Many hotels, travel agencies, tour operators, air carriers and other service providers in tourism can feel the pinch already.

Do people still need to go on holiday? If so, where to and what is affordable? Can expensive lodges, up market hotels, exclusive operators and glittery excursions still survive during these difficult times? If not, what are the alternatives?

One such an alternative is to go on holiday on a train tour – either on a budget or an upmarket holiday, sport or festival train tour. The variety of train tours hosted by JB Train Tours in Southern Africa, at an affordable rate in a comfortable, safe and secure environment, have changed the way and view of going on holiday of many South Africans as well as visitors from abroad.
 

A train tour holiday is currently one of the most affordable holidays and value for money in South Africa, suitable for all ages, people from all walks of life, as well as for the individual, family or corporate companies.

Think about it, consider the facts, book a place and enjoy it.
