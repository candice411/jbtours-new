CONFED CUP GONE – 2010 FIFA WORLD CUP COMING

The world is talking about the 2010 FIFA World Cup soccer tournament to be hosted in South Africa next year. And so are many local people as well. It is such a huge project involving so many people, organisations and governments. What a pleasure and privilege to be part of it – in our own country.

The Confederations Cup was played in South Africa in June and what a festival and spectacle it was. Did you attend one or more of the games? I did – and it was great fun. Are you going to attend at least one game next year? Please do so. You won’t have the opportunity to be able to attend it again in your home country – ever.

Why the above two paragraphs? We currently live in times of lots of negativity, doom and tough financial times, though there are lots of opportunities – some of them unique. Make use of it and cash in.
 


In the mean time you can travel with us on one of our many upmarket or budget train tours to a variety of places in South Africa at prices very affordable and facilities very comfortable. The reduced price on the Namibian train tours in September 2009 should not be missed. This is a tour not to be repeated at this discounted price.

Enjoy the month and take the opportunities.

Travel Greetings
Johan Badenhorst & the JB Train Tours team

August 2009
Event: Joy of Jazz
Where: Johannesburg, Gauteng
Date: 27 – 29 August 2009
Website: www.joyofjazz.co.za
More info: Johannesburg's biggest annual jazz festival is an ideal family outing, featuring a range of musical styles but with a strong emphasis on jazz. Over 200 local and international artists perform at different venues across the city, particularly in Newtown.
Train ticket from CTN to JHB: R1 520pp
Train ticket from DBN to JHB: R720pp
(Price based on Premier Classe train, one way)
   
September 2009
Event: Aardklop Arts Festival
Where: Potchefstroom, North West (en route from CTN – JHB)
Date: 29 September – 3 October 2009
Website: www.aardklop.co.za
More info: Aardklop Arts Festival offers a feast of arts and an all-round good jol for five days in late September and early October. First held in 1998, Aardklop - Afrikaans roughly translated as "earth beat" - has over 90 productions, with classical music, jazz, hard rock, cabaret, visual arts, theatre, circus performances, opera, African and World music, poetry and more, ending with the OppiAarde rock festival on the final day.
Train ticket from CTN to JHB: R1 920pp
Train ticket from DBN to JHB: R870pp
(Price based on Premier Classe train, one way)
